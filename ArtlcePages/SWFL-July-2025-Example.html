SWFL July 2025: Bonita-Estero vs Naples vs Fort Myers

Author: Bonita Springs-Estero REALTORS® • Published 2025-08-14

Southwest Florida’s July 2025 market shows higher inventory and steadier pricing. Buyers now have choices across Bonita Springs-Estero, Naples, and Fort Myers. Below we compare key trends, focusing on the inventory surge and what it means for your next move.

Inventory is up across the board

The biggest story is supply. Active listings rose sharply from mid-2021 lows, giving shoppers room to compare neighborhoods and negotiate. Year over year in July, homes for sale increased +21.4% in Bonita Springs-Estero, +12.8% in Naples, and +17.0% in Fort Myers.

Marketing times reflect a normalizing market. Median days on market reached 83 in Bonita Springs-Estero, 85 in Naples, and 66 in Fort Myers. Buyers can take a breath; sellers should plan for longer exposure than during 2021–2022.

How each market compares in July 2025

Bonita Springs-Estero: Closed sales jumped +18.0% and pending sales surged +36.5% year over year. The median price settled near $495,000 (-11.6% YoY). New listings rose +8.1%, while total homes for sale climbed +21.4%. Buyers responded to improved affordability and selection.

Naples: The median price held $570,000 (flat year over year), supported by cash and luxury activity. Pending sales increased +23.9% and inventory rose +12.8%. New listings edged up +2.4%, keeping options healthy without destabilizing prices.

Fort Myers: Median price was $335,000 (-8.2% YoY). Pending sales improved +11.0% despite fewer new listings (-3.8%). Total homes for sale still grew +17.0%, signaling slower turnover and the need for sharp pricing.

What it means for buyers

Inventory gives you leverage. Compare multiple homes, scrutinize disclosures, and negotiate repairs or credits. Focus on condition and location. Updated homes in prime neighborhoods still move quickly when priced right.

What it means for sellers

Competition is back. Price to current comparable sales, complete pre-listing repairs, and stage for impact. Expect longer market times than during the frenzy, but motivated buyers are active across SWFL—especially for well-presented homes.

Key takeaways

  • Inventory increased 13–21% year over year across the three areas.
  • Median prices are steady to slightly lower; Naples held firm, Bonita and Fort Myers adjusted.
  • Marketing times lengthened, signaling a balanced market with better options for buyers.

Sources: Bonita Springs-Estero REALTORS® July 2025 reports and area infographics. Values above reflect year-over-year changes for July 2025.
