🏠 For Homeowners & Consumers

Warning Signs & Red Flags

Read more about common warning signs
  • Unexpected recorded documents in the mail.
  • Forged signature on a deed or mortgage.
  • Ownership transferred without your knowledge.
  • Loans or HELOC activity you didn't initiate.
  • Stopped tax bills or mail.
  • Foreclosure or default notices when your accounts are current.
Source: Lee Clerk "Property Fraud Alert Registration Tips"

What to do if you suspect fraud

  • Step 1: Report the suspected crime to local law enforcement immediately.
  • Step 2: Consider retaining a real estate attorney to file a Quiet Title action in civil court.
  • Step 3: Record a Notice of Lis Pendens (if applicable) during civil litigation to notify the public.
  • Step 4: Contact your Property Appraiser and Tax Collector if your mailing address was changed.
  • Step 5: Check records and sign up for Clerk fraud alerts in all counties where you own property.
Source: Lee County Clerk "Quiet Title for Fraudulent Conveyance" packet

💼 For REALTORS® & Industry Partners

Wire Fraud / Closing Payment Safety

  • Verify Changes Personally: Always verify any change in wiring instructions by calling a known, trusted phone number (not one embedded in the email).
  • Use MFA: Implement robust multi-factor authentication across all accounts.
  • Beware of Urgency: Scammers manufacture urgency around closings and strike during holidays or weekends. Inspect sender addresses carefully.
Source: FBI BEC Warning & Collier Clerk Advisory

Why fraud can still get recorded

By Florida law, Clerks record documents that meet statutory recording requirements. The clerk does not verify the legal validity of signatures or the truthfulness of the content prior to recording. Clearing the title typically requires court action.

Key Florida Legal Statutes

  • F.S. 817.535: Criminalizes filing materially false instruments with intent to defraud/harass.
  • F.S. 65.091: Creates an action to quiet title based on fraudulent attempted conveyances.
  • F.S. 695.26: Mandates that instruments affecting real property closely print the names and post office addresses for each signer and witness.
Source: Florida Statutes

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this prevent someone from selling my home?
No. The service serves solely as an early warning system. It will send you a prompt notification when a document matching your registered name or parcel/STRAP is officially recorded. It does not freeze your title or prevent presentation of documents. If you receive an alert and the recording is suspicious, you must immediately investigate and contact law enforcement.
Can I sign up if I don't own property in the county?
Yes. Many clerk systems allow you to monitor name variations regardless of current ownership.
Are my signup details public record?
No. Florida HB 285 (May 6, 2024) created a public-records exemption for info submitted to clerks for recording notification services.
