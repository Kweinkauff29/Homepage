      1. Canada

      Market Snapshot

      • Top buyer country in FL by sales share.
      • Cash heavy: ~87% buy all-cash (vs. ~67% overall FL).
      • Popular metros include Naples–Marco, Cape Coral–Fort Myers, Punta Gorda (plus Miami).
      • Common property use: winter/vacation; condos and SFH both in play.

      Agent Playbook

      • Prep FIRPTA + tax-pro referrals early (sale/withholding planning).
      • Quote costs in USD and CAD; offer a trusted FX partner.
      • Streamline remote closings and insurance setup; outline auto/vehicle & HOA rules for snowbirds.

      Tip: Canadians skew non-resident (Type-A) buyers, so U.S. mortgages are uncommon; focus on proof-of-funds, wire-safety, and condo association approvals.

      2. United Kingdom

      Market Snapshot

      • Among FL’s top 5 buyer countries by sales.
      • Shift toward single-family homes (about three-quarters of UK buyers).
      • Primary uses: vacation + some primary residence moves.

      Agent Playbook

      • Clarify HOA, insurance, reserves & post-2022 condo reforms when comparing SFH vs. condo.
      • Provide rental-rule summaries (short-term limits vary widely).
      • Offer guidance on bank accounts, SIM/credit, and travel-insurance for extended stays.

      3. Europe

      Market Snapshot

      • Consistent demand from Germany, France, Italy & Nordics.
      • Value energy-efficiency, building standards, storm-hardening.
      • Urban/coastal preference; many buy for vacation + occasional rental.

      Agent Playbook

      • Provide condo reserves, insurance deductibles, and wind/flood details up front.
      • Expect document requests (spec sheets, seller disclosures, 3- to 5-year HOA budgets).
      • Offer wire-fraud education and remote-notary workflows.

      4. Asia/Pacific

      Market Snapshot

      • Active groups: Japan, Korea, India, Australia; mix of new-build and turnkey condos.
      • Often remote closings with strict bank transfer limits and ID checks.
      • Student-use and second homes present in university/tech corridors.

      Agent Playbook

      • Provide step-by-step cross-border wire instructions and 2FA escrow processes.
      • Have lender options for non-resident borrowers; outline seasoning & reserve rules.
      • Offer bilingual summaries and name-order confirmation for contracts/wires.

      5. Latin America

      Market Snapshot

      • Top-five countries include Argentina, Colombia, Brazil.
      • Price tiers: Brazilians trend higher (median among highest); Argentines buy more condos; Colombians split use between vacation & rental.
      • Urban/coastal preference (Miami-tri-county dominates, but SWFL sees Canadian/British/LATAM demand).

      Agent Playbook

      • Track FX moves; time wires and walkthroughs around currency swings.
      • Clarify rental rules, licensing, and condo approval timelines.
      • Collect apostilles early for POAs/passports; plan for in-person/remote notarization.
      Median price for Florida international buyers was ~$469K (2024 window) and cash share ~67%—well above statewide averages. Expect strong interest in $500K–$750K segments and premium waterfront.

      Global Business & Culture Tips

      China

      • Provide bilingual docs (English + Mandarin) and confirm name order for contracts and wires.
      • They often expect multiple levels of review — be patient and precise.
      • Avoid direct “yes/no” until agreement is finalized — they might defer initially.
      • Highlight security, HOA rules, flood risk; visuals of community layout help.
      • Remote closing is often a must; ensure escrow, notary, and apostille flows are clear.

      India

      • Trust is key — offer references, testimonials, and detailed credentials.
      • Many Indian buyers expect to rent out; clarify local tax, short-term rules, and yield projections.
      • Be ready for multiple decision points — sometimes families decide together from abroad.
      • Present comparisons (Sqft, cost per unit) — Indian clients often like side-by-side metrics.
      • Provide clarity on visas, banking, repatriation, and capital gains rules.

      Mexico

      • Build rapport, address their concerns about legal title and property rights.
      • Provide Spanish versions of key docs; translations + POAs help smooth process.
      • Clarify how U.S. mortgages vs all-cash offers work locally.
      • Explain HOA, flood insurance, hurricane prep clearly — many buyers unfamiliar with U.S. standards.
      • Use visual comparisons to properties in Mexico — helps ground expectations.

      Canada

      • Taxes: explain FIRPTA withholding, nonresident returns, capital gains.
      • Provide dual currencies (USD & CAD) in all key documents/estimates.
      • Many Canadian buyers expect cash deals — have proof-of-funds protocols ready.
      • Snowbird logistics: mail, insurance, vehicle rules, utilities setup.
      • Condo rules: short-term rental, reserve funds, special assessments — make them transparent.

      United Kingdom

      • Emphasize stability, regulatory transparency, and property right protections.
      • Flood and storm risk matter; show mitigation features, elevation, wind-resistance specs.
      • Rental potential: many expect to rent when not using. Show comps & legal limits.
      • Explain closing costs (stamp duty equivalents) and U.S. property taxes.
      • Offer GBP ↔ USD pricing and periodic exchange check snapshots.

      Europe (Continental)

      • Varied buyer cultures: Germans favor precision, Italians appreciate narrative storytelling.
      • Energy & insulation: European buyers often expect high performance, so share specs & utility data.
      • Condo governance: budgets, reserve, debt history, insurance transparency matter here.
      • Rental/vacation use: many own vacation property — show tourist rental zoning, HOA rules.
      • Offer floor plans with metric ↔ imperial conversions, and local comparables.

      Asia / Pacific

      • Safety, security, maintenance: many want low-maintenance properties in gated communities.
      • Modern finishes and turn-key delivery are highly valued — highlight premium features.
      • Remote closing and escrow: many won’t travel — ensure digital workflows are clear.
      • Bank transfer constraints and foreign exchange quirks need step-by-step guidance.
      • Explain hurricane/flood backup systems, generator options, structural safeguards.

      Latin America

      • Currency risk: timing wires matters. Offer escrow paths or forward contracts when possible.
      • Trust: use referrals, client stories, bilingual team members.
      • Documents: ensure apostilles, POAs, translations are ready early.
      • Rental expectations: many expect high yields; show comps, vacancy data, licensing rules.
      • Political volatility: buyers like reassurance around long-term stability and exit strategies.
